# Brand Voice — Fort Myers Auto Glass

*(Working name + phone are TODO placeholders. Mobile auto glass / windshield business,
Fort Myers + Southwest Florida. Every page — services, locations — follows this file so
the whole site reads as one company.)*

## Voice in a line
A friendly, straight-talking local glass pro — reassuring and insurance-savvy, never hypey.
We make a stressful day (cracked windshield, mobile booking, insurance) feel easy.

## Person / POV
"We" (the company) talking to "you" (the **driver / vehicle owner**).
**Never** "homeowner," "contractor," "property," "project," or "materials" — this is an
**automotive, mobile** business, not a home-services trade. Say *vehicle, windshield, glass,
chip, crack, install, come to you, claim*.

## Reading level
~Grade 7–8. Short sentences. Concrete over abstract. Lead with the driver's problem.

## Do
- Lead with the problem/benefit ("Chip in your windshield? We come to you.").
- Be specific to auto glass + Southwest Florida (heat, love bugs, I-75 debris).
- Reassure: mobile (we come to you), insured, insurance claims welcome, free quote, ADAS done right.
- Active voice. Scannable: short paragraphs, bullets, a few **bold** key phrases, repeated CTAs (call / free quote).

## Don't
- Clichés ("top-notch," "we go above and beyond"), hype, or filler.
- Contractor/home-services wording (see POV above).
- **Don't claim "licensed"** — auto glass isn't a licensed trade in FL. "Insured" is the honest claim.
- **FL windshield-insurance** (verified): Florida historically required a **$0 deductible** on windshield
  replacement under comprehensive coverage (**FL Statute 627.7288**). A **2023 overhaul** now lets insurers
  offer policies *without* the zero deductible (in exchange for a discount). So **never assert "free with
  insurance" flatly** — it now depends on the driver's specific policy. Correct framing: "Florida has long
  been a no-deductible state, but a 2023 change means it depends on your policy — we'll help you check."

## Claims hygiene (hard rule)
Never invent reviews, ratings, star counts, years in business, job counts, awards, or dollar
figures. Qualitative is fine ("locally owned," "experienced," "mobile"). Fabricated numbers are not.
REVIEWS ship empty until real ones exist.

## Trade vocabulary (use naturally — sounds expert)
Windshield, windshield repair vs. replacement, rock chip / star break / bullseye, crack spread,
OEM vs. aftermarket glass, urethane adhesive, **safe drive-away time**, ADAS calibration
(lane-departure / auto-braking cameras), back glass, door/quarter glass, mobile install,
insurance claim / deductible.

## SEO copy standards (all skills follow)
- **One page, one primary keyword + intent.** Map before writing:
  - Homepage = **"auto glass Fort Myers"** / "mobile auto glass Fort Myers" (intent: hire mobile auto glass).
  - Service page = **"{service} in Fort Myers"** (e.g. "windshield replacement in Fort Myers").
  - City page = **"auto glass in {city}"** / "mobile windshield repair {city}".
  - Add 2–3 secondary terms per page (rock chip repair, windshield replacement cost, ADAS calibration, insurance claim).
- **Headings:** exactly one H1 with the primary keyword (natural phrasing). H2/H3 use secondary terms + real questions (People Also Ask). Never stuff.
- **Meta:** title ~50–60 chars (keyword + Fort Myers + brand); description ~150–160 with a benefit + CTA. Unique per page.
- **Internal linking (priority):** hub-and-spoke (home → /services → each service; /service-area → each city). Cross-link service↔service and service↔city (city pages list popular services; service bodies name key cities). VARIED, descriptive anchors ("windshield replacement," not "click here" or the same exact phrase). No orphans; link the free-quote CTA in body copy.
- **E-E-A-T — show it:** Experience = first-hand SW FL specifics (heat, love-bug season, I-75 debris); Expertise = correct method/materials/why (urethane cure, safe drive-away, ADAS); Authority = insured, insurance claims welcome, workmanship guarantee (state what's true); Trust = honest factors (no fake prices), real reviews only, consistent NAP, clear mobile service area.
- **Hyper-local authenticity:** name real roads, landmarks, and areas (see Knowledge Bank); reference heat/UV speeding crack spread, love bugs, afternoon storms, snowbird season. For a **mobile** business there's no permitting/HOA angle — lean on roads, driving conditions, and where we come to you.
- **Unique content per page (critical, anti-doorway):** never reuse paragraphs across service or city pages. Swapping only the city name = a doorway page. Each page carries distinct, useful local + service detail.
- **Topical completeness:** cover repair vs. replace, process, ADAS, insurance, cost factors (no fake numbers), FAQs.
- **NAP consistency:** name / phone identical everywhere (once the real ones replace the placeholders). Mobile business → service-area, no street address.

## Local Knowledge Bank (auto-glass-relevant; verify neighborhood-level specifics)
**Region-wide (Southwest Florida / Lee County):**
- Intense heat + UV: a small chip can spread into a full crack **fast** — repair early.
- **Love-bug season** (≈May & Sept): swarms splatter/pit windshields.
- Afternoon thunderstorms; daily highway driving on **I-75** and **US-41 (Tamiami Trail / Cleveland Ave)** kicks up gravel/road debris → rock chips.
- Big **snowbird / retiree** population; **Nov–Apr** seasonal traffic surge. Hurricane Ian (2022) debris still a factor.

**Fort Myers** — I-75, US-41, **Colonial Blvd, Daniels Pkwy, Summerlin Rd, McGregor Blvd**, downtown River District, near RSW airport. Lee County.
**Cape Coral** — canal city, **Pine Island Rd, Del Prado Blvd, Cape Coral Pkwy, Veterans Pkwy**; toll bridges (Cape Coral, Midpoint); many retirees.
**Lehigh Acres** — sprawling, lots of new construction (road debris) — **Lee Blvd, Homestead Rd, Sunshine Blvd**.
**Estero** — I-75 corridor, **Coconut Point, Miromar Outlets, Corkscrew Rd**, FGCU nearby.
**Bonita Springs** — **Bonita Beach Rd, US-41, I-75**; Lee/Collier line; heavy snowbird season.
**North Fort Myers** — **US-41, Bayshore Rd, Pine Island Rd**; Caloosahatchee bridges into downtown.
**Fort Myers Beach** — Estero Island, **San Carlos Blvd, Matanzas Pass bridge**; salt air; rebuilding post-Ian.
