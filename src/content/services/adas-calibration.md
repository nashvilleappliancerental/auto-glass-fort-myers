If your car warns you when you drift out of a lane or brakes on its own to avoid a collision, those features run on a camera mounted to your windshield. Replace the glass and that camera has to be re-aimed — that's **ADAS calibration**, and it's the step that makes a windshield replacement genuinely safe on a modern vehicle.

## A small aim error becomes a big one

ADAS stands for Advanced Driver Assistance Systems — the cameras and sensors behind lane-keeping, adaptive cruise, and automatic braking. They're aimed with tight tolerances. Move the camera a hair during a [windshield replacement](/windshield-replacement) and that tiny shift translates into a large error a few hundred feet down the road, right where the system is supposed to react. Calibration resets the aim to the manufacturer's specification.
